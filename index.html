Did you think this was going to last forever?

I certainly did.

If there's one thing that this comic has taught me, over and over again, it's that change happens.

Sometimes it's finding out your syllabus had a typo and finals are actually next week.

But sometimes it means moving on from something you've grown to love.

I created The Alder Groove back in 2016 as a fun inside joke; never really expecting it to outlive Algebra I, much less become the face of the school.

Early comics consisted of me and a few of my friends roasting "the establishment" - but by the end of 2019, Merlin's face was on an inspirational poster.

I enjoyed every minute that I put my pencil to the paper. It was a way for me to get creative, to vent, maybe work in an easter egg or two.

And I know that a lot of you out there are reading this and thinking, "What the heck, man?" Maybe you can't even find the words, you're so disappointed.

The Alder Groove was never intended to be permanent. That's what this is all about - me, stepping aside and letting those inspired by my work pick up on it.

It's up to the next batch of bewildered freshmen to churn out the funnies, and I'm curious to see what they come up with.

But as for me, I'd like to move on. I'd like to find new experiences, meet new people, go do some cool stuff elsewhere.

If it's okay with all of you, I'd like to close the book and open another one.

So thanks.

To all of you who critiqued and proofread my drafts, thanks. And to all of you who laughed at the final result, thanks.

But most importantly, thank you for your support.
