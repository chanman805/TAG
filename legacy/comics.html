"If at first you don't succeed, just pretend like you did. It has the same effect." My own personal spin on the famous over-used phrase, it's a mantra for my work here. Whether it's to do with subject matter, art quality, or just my overall feelings towards it, these comics here are the ones that are keepers, for sure, but don't really measure up to the others. Leaf through them and tell me what you think, enjoy!

This is a collection that I'm still not sure what to make of. I set out to answer the question, "What happens to high-schoolers when there's no school?" Turns out, not much: Nowhere near enough to keep the strip going, anyway. I got a handful of good strips, but the rest are pretty much garbage to me. Sort through the rubble to find your favorite, but I'm not planning on starting this back up anytime soon, so don't expect any more of these. Enjoy!

Just what it sounds like - favorites chosen by you, the fans. Some of my best work is in this collection, whether it's the art style, perfect comedic timing, or randomness of the particular strip. I've heard that art is subjective, and I can tell you that those words apply to humor as well... In other words, take a look, tell me what you think, and who knows? I'll be filtering the collection as I come up with new work, so you may see your favorite comic here as a result!

What you'll read is, more or less, what I run into in high school. This collection predominantly deals with school drama. All the people and places, sure, but most predominantly, the assignments. Which is weird, because it's what you're least likely to remember ten, fifteen years down the road...anyway, this was what the comic covered in its early days. Pencils, paper, graphing calculators, and complaining. A lovely recipie for strip material.
